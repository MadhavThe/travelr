Varanasi, also known as Banaras or Kashi, is one of the oldest and holiest cities in India, located in the northern state of Uttar Pradesh. Its history is rich and deeply intertwined with Indian culture, spirituality, and religion.

Coordinates: Latitude: 25.3176° N, Longitude: 82.9739° E

History:

The history of Varanasi dates back thousands of years, making it one of the world's oldest continually inhabited cities. It is often referred to as the "Spiritual Capital of India" and the "City of Temples."

Ancient Roots:

Varanasi's history can be traced back to the Vedic period (around 1500 BCE), making it one of the seven holiest cities in Hinduism. It is believed to be founded by Lord Shiva, and the city is closely associated with several Hindu scriptures.

Buddhism:

Gautama Buddha is said to have delivered his first sermon in Sarnath, located just a few kilometers from Varanasi. This event marked the beginning of Buddhism, and Sarnath remains an essential pilgrimage site for Buddhists.

Medieval Period:

During the medieval period, Varanasi was a center of learning and culture. It attracted scholars, poets, and artists, contributing significantly to the development of classical music, dance, and literature.

Islamic Rule:

The city experienced multiple periods of Islamic rule, including the Ghaznavid and Mughal empires. Many Islamic monuments, including mosques and tombs, were built during this time.

British Era:

The British East India Company took control of Varanasi in the 18th century. It played a vital role in India's struggle for independence, with several prominent leaders emerging from the city.

Famous Places in Varanasi:

  • Kashi Vishwanath Temple: Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this temple is one of the most sacred in Hinduism. It is known for its stunning architecture and intricate carvings.
  • Ganges River Ghats: Varanasi is famous for its numerous ghats along the Ganges River. The most notable is the Dashashwamedh Ghat, where the Ganga Aarti (ritual of worship) takes place daily.
  • Sarnath: This is where Buddha delivered his first sermon. The Dhamek Stupa and the Sarnath Archaeological Museum are key attractions here.
  • Benaras Hindu University (BHU): Founded by Pandit Madan Mohan Malaviya, BHU is one of India's premier educational institutions. The campus features beautiful architecture, including the Bharat Kala Bhavan museum.
  • Ramnagar Fort: Located on the eastern bank of the Ganges, this fort is the ancestral home of the Maharaja of Varanasi. It houses a museum displaying vintage cars, royal costumes, and more.
  • Tulsi Manas Temple: Dedicated to Lord Rama, this temple is known for its walls inscribed with verses from the Ramcharitmanas, an epic poem on the life of Rama.
  • Bharat Mata Temple: This unique temple is dedicated to Mother India and features a large relief map of undivided India made of marble.
  • Assi Ghat: It's one of the southernmost ghats in Varanasi and a popular spot for taking boat rides and witnessing the sunrise.
  • Gyanvapi Mosque: A prominent Islamic structure, it is located near the Kashi Vishwanath Temple and reflects the city's multicultural heritage.
