History:

Chandela Dynasty: The temples were constructed during the rule of the Chandela dynasty, which was known for its patronage of the arts and culture. It's believed that there were originally around 85 temples in Khajuraho, but only about 25 have survived over the centuries.

Decline and Rediscovery: Over time, the temples fell into disuse and were gradually abandoned. They were hidden from the world by dense forests until they were rediscovered by British archaeologists in the 19th century. Since then, efforts have been made to restore and preserve these magnificent structures.

Places to Visit:

1. Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds): This iconic palace is known for its distinctive pink facade with intricately designed windows. It was built for the royal women to observe street festivals while maintaining their privacy.

2. City Palace: The City Palace complex is a sprawling royal residence with museums, courtyards, and palatial buildings. The Chandra Mahal and Mubarak Mahal within the complex are must-visit attractions.

Significance:

Historical Capital: Jaipur was founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II as the new capital of the Kachwaha Rajput dynasty. It was established with the guidance of Vidyadhar Bhattacharya, a Bengali architect, and was one of India's first planned cities.

Architectural Marvel: The city is renowned for its stunning architecture, with many historic palaces, forts, and temples. The blend of Rajput and Mughal architectural styles is evident throughout the city.
