History:

Chandela Dynasty: The temples were constructed during the rule of the Chandela dynasty, which was known for its patronage of the arts and culture. It's believed that there were originally around 85 temples in Khajuraho, but only about 25 have survived over the centuries.

Decline and Rediscovery: Over time, the temples fell into disuse and were gradually abandoned. They were hidden from the world by dense forests until they were rediscovered by British archaeologists in the 19th century. Since then, efforts have been made to restore and preserve these magnificent structures.

Nearby Places to Visit:

1. Virupaksha Temple: This ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva is one of the most iconic landmarks in Hampi and a major pilgrimage site.

2. Vittala Temple: Known for its stunning stone chariot and musical pillars, this temple is a masterpiece of Vijayanagara architecture.

Significance:

Historical Importance: Hampi was the capital of the mighty Vijayanagara Empire, which ruled Southern India from the 14th to the 16th century. The ruins of this once-prosperous city now stand as a testament to its glorious past and architectural grandeur.

Architectural Marvel: The town is home to a vast complex of temples, palaces, and other structures that showcase exceptional Dravidian architecture. The Virupaksha Temple and Vittala Temple are among the most famous and intricate examples.
