About Periyar Tiger Reserve:

The Periyar Tiger Reserve is a well-known protected area located in the Indian state of Kerala. It is renowned for its biodiversity, lush forests, and the conservation of the Bengal tiger. The reserve, established in 1978, is part of the Western Ghats, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its ecological importance.

History:

The Periyar Tiger Reserve was initially named the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary and was established in 1978. It was later upgraded to a tiger reserve due to its importance in tiger conservation. The reserve is named after the Periyar River and is part of the Western Ghats, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Significance:

  • Tiger Conservation: The reserve is known for its efforts to conserve the Bengal tiger and is one of the 51 tiger reserves in India.
  • Biodiversity: Periyar is rich in biodiversity, hosting numerous animal and plant species, including several endangered and endemic species.
  • Tourism and Awareness: The reserve attracts eco-tourists, nature enthusiasts, and researchers, contributing to wildlife conservation awareness.
  • Periyar Lake: The scenic Periyar Lake, formed by the Mullaperiyar Dam, offers boat rides for visitors to enjoy the natural beauty and spot wildlife from a safe distance.

Specialties:

  • Wildlife: In addition to tigers, the reserve is home to diverse fauna, including elephants, leopards, gaur (Indian bison), sambar deer, Nilgiri langurs, and numerous bird species.
  • Flora: The reserve features a variety of vegetation types, including evergreen forests, deciduous forests, and grasslands. It's known for its diverse plant species.
  • Boat Safaris: Tourists can take boat safaris on Periyar Lake to observe wildlife, especially during the dry season when animals gather near the water source.
  • Bamboo Rafting: Bamboo rafting is a unique adventure activity offered in the reserve, allowing visitors to explore the forest along with trained guides.

Nearby Places:

  • Thekkady: Thekkady is the nearest town to the Periyar Tiger Reserve and serves as a base for tourists. It offers various accommodations, restaurants, and shopping options.
  • Kumily: Located near Thekkady, Kumily is famous for its spice plantations and spice markets. Visitors can explore the spice gardens and purchase fresh spices.
  • Mullaperiyar Dam: The Mullaperiyar Dam, which creates the Periyar Lake, is an engineering marvel and a notable attraction for those interested in dams and reservoirs.
  • Gavi: Gavi, a serene eco-tourism spot in the reserve, is known for its untouched forests, birdwatching, and nature walks.

Location:

The Periyar Tiger Reserve is situated at approximately 9.4075° N latitude and 77.2531° E longitude in the state of Kerala, India.
