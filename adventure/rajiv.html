History:

The Rajiv Gandhi Zoological Park was established in 1999. It covers an area of approximately 165 acres and is located in the Katraj suburb of Pune.

Significance:

  • Wildlife Conservation: The zoo plays a crucial role in the conservation of various animal species, including some that are endangered or vulnerable.
  • Education and Awareness: It serves as an educational center where visitors can learn about different animals, their habitats, and the importance of wildlife conservation.
  • Recreational Space: Apart from its conservation efforts, the zoo provides a recreational space for families and tourists to enjoy a day outdoors while observing animals from around the world.

Specialties:

  • Animal Exhibits: The zoo features a diverse range of animal exhibits, including lions, tigers, leopards, elephants, rhinoceroses, reptiles, birds, and numerous other species.
  • Safari: The zoo offers a "zoo safari" experience, where visitors can see animals in a semi-natural setting.
  • Butterfly Garden: There is a butterfly garden within the zoo, showcasing various species of butterflies.
  • Walking Trails: Visitors can explore the zoo on foot, enjoying the lush greenery and beautiful landscaping.

Nearby Places:

  • Peshwa Udyan: Also known as the Saras Baug, this historic garden and temple complex is located nearby and is a peaceful place for a leisurely stroll.
  • Parvati Hill: Parvati Hill offers panoramic views of Pune and houses the Parvati Temple, attracting both tourists and pilgrims.
  • Shaniwar Wada: This historic fortification and palace in Pune is a popular historical site and is not far from the zoo.

Location:

The Rajiv Gandhi Zoological Park is situated at approximately 18.6189° N latitude and 73.8298° E longitude in Pune, Maharashtra, India.
