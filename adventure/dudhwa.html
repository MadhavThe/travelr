About Dudhwa National Park:

Dudhwa National Park is a prominent protected area located in the state of Uttar Pradesh, India. This national park is known for its diverse wildlife, particularly the Indian rhinoceros, and its rich natural beauty. The park, established in 1958 as a wildlife sanctuary, was designated as a national park in 1977 and became part of the Dudhwa Tiger Reserve in 1987 to facilitate the conservation of the Bengal tiger.

History:

Dudhwa National Park was established in 1958 as a wildlife sanctuary. In 1977, it was designated as a national park and later became part of the Dudhwa Tiger Reserve in 1987 to facilitate the conservation of the Bengal tiger.

Significance:

  • Conservation: The park is primarily dedicated to the conservation and protection of endangered species, including the Indian rhinoceros, tigers, leopards, swamp deer (barasingha), and various other mammals, reptiles, and avian species.
  • Biodiversity: Dudhwa is renowned for its rich biodiversity and is a vital part of the Terai-Duar savanna and grasslands ecoregion, which spans across India and Nepal.
  • Rhino Conservation: It's one of the few places in India where the Indian rhinoceros is found, and the park plays a critical role in the conservation of this species.

Specialties:

  • Indian Rhinoceros: Dudhwa is famous for its successful efforts in protecting and breeding the Indian rhinoceros. The park has a significant population of these rhinos.
  • Barasingha: The park is also home to the barasingha, a unique swamp deer species, and it is often referred to as the "land of the barasingha."
  • Tigers and Leopards: Dudhwa Tiger Reserve within the national park houses Bengal tigers and leopards, making it a prime destination for tiger conservation and safaris.
  • Avian Diversity: The park is a birdwatcher's paradise with a variety of bird species, including the endangered Bengal florican.

Nearby Places:

  • Lakhimpur: Lakhimpur Kheri is the nearest town to Dudhwa National Park and serves as a gateway for tourists. It offers accommodations and other amenities.
  • Katarniaghat Wildlife Sanctuary: This sanctuary is nearby and is known for its diverse wildlife, including gharials (a type of crocodile) and various bird species.
  • Kishanpur Wildlife Sanctuary: Another sanctuary in the vicinity, Kishanpur is home to tigers, leopards, and a wide variety of wildlife.
  • Nepal Border: Dudhwa shares its border with Nepal, and there is a border crossing point for those interested in exploring the international border region.

Location:

Dudhwa National Park is situated at approximately 28.4450° N latitude and 80.6146° E longitude in the state of Uttar Pradesh, India.
