History:

Bannerghatta Biological Park was established in 1970, primarily with the aim of conserving and protecting wildlife. It covers an expansive area of approximately 104.27 square kilometers and encompasses a variety of ecosystems, including dry deciduous forests and scrublands.

Location:

Bannerghatta Biological Park is situated at approximately 12.8000° N latitude and 77.5773° E longitude in Bangalore, Karnataka, India.

Significance:

  • Wildlife Conservation: The park is dedicated to the conservation of various animal species, especially those that are native to the region or endangered.
  • Education and Research: It serves as an educational and research center, promoting awareness about wildlife conservation and providing opportunities for research and study.
  • Recreation: The park offers recreational activities and experiences for visitors, making it a popular family destination.

Specialties:

  • Animal Exhibits: Bannerghatta Biological Park features a diverse collection of animals, including tigers, lions, leopards, elephants, rhinoceroses, reptiles, and a variety of bird species.
  • Safari Options: Visitors can embark on safaris within the park, including a safari through a lion and tiger reserve and a herbivore safari.
  • Butterfly Park: The park includes a butterfly conservatory, where visitors can observe and learn about different species of butterflies.
  • Rescue and Rehabilitation: The park also has a rescue and rehabilitation center for animals, particularly those that have been injured or orphaned in the wild.

Nearby Places:

  • Bangalore City: The park is located on the outskirts of Bangalore, and visitors can explore the city's vibrant culture, dining, shopping, and other attractions.
  • Bannerghatta National Park: While the biological park is the primary attraction, Bannerghatta National Park, which was the original protected area, is also worth exploring for its natural beauty and wildlife.
  • Art of Living International Center: This spiritual and cultural center, founded by Sri Sri Ravi Shankar, is located nearby and offers programs, meditation, and retreats.
  • Wonderla Amusement Park: Wonderla, one of India's leading amusement parks, is not far from Bannerghatta and is a fun-filled destination for families.
