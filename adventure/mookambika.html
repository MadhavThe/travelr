About Mookambika Wildlife Sanctuary:

The Mookambika Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected natural area located in the state of Karnataka, India. It spans over a vast area and is known for its rich biodiversity and stunning natural beauty. The sanctuary was established in 1974 with the primary goal of conserving the unique flora and fauna of the Western Ghats, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

History:

The Mookambika Wildlife Sanctuary has a relatively recent history, with its establishment dating back to the year 1974. It was created to protect the rich biodiversity of the Western Ghats and to ensure the conservation of the region's unique ecosystems. Over the years, it has become an important sanctuary for the preservation of various plant and animal species.

Significance:

  • Biodiversity: The sanctuary is known for its rich biodiversity, housing a wide variety of plant and animal species, including several that are endangered or endemic to the Western Ghats.
  • Conservation: It plays a crucial role in the conservation of the Western Ghats' unique ecosystems, including tropical rainforests, evergreen forests, and moist deciduous forests.
  • Habitat: The sanctuary provides a safe haven for diverse wildlife, including elephants, tigers, leopards, and various species of deer, as well as numerous bird species and reptiles.
  • Research: Researchers and conservationists visit the sanctuary to study and monitor the Western Ghats' ecology and wildlife.

Specialties:

  • Flora: The sanctuary is known for its lush vegetation, including diverse species of trees, ferns, orchids, and medicinal plants.
  • Fauna: It is home to a variety of wildlife, including the endangered lion-tailed macaque, Malabar giant squirrel, and numerous species of amphibians and reptiles.
  • Avian Diversity: Birdwatchers can enjoy spotting various bird species, such as the Malabar trogon, great hornbill, and Indian pitta.
  • Scenic Beauty: The sanctuary offers breathtaking natural landscapes, with streams, waterfalls, and dense forests, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers.
