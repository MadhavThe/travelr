History:

Bandipur's history as a protected area dates back to 1931 when it was established as a game reserve by the Maharaja of the Kingdom of Mysore. It was later declared a national park in 1974. The park has a rich history of wildlife conservation and has played a pivotal role in the Project Tiger initiative launched in 1973.

Places Nearby to Visit:

1. Mudumalai National Park: Located in Tamil Nadu, adjacent to Bandipur, Mudumalai is another important wildlife sanctuary known for its diverse flora and fauna.

2. Nagarhole National Park: Also known as Rajiv Gandhi National Park, it is located nearby and is renowned for its thriving elephant population.

3. Ooty: A famous hill station in Tamil Nadu, Ooty is not far from Bandipur and is known for its picturesque landscapes and botanical gardens.

4. Coorg (Kodagu): A beautiful hill station in Karnataka, Coorg is known for its coffee plantations and serene landscapes.

5. Wayanad: Located in Kerala, Wayanad is known for its wildlife sanctuaries, trekking trails, and beautiful scenery.

6. Mysore: The historic city of Mysore is about 80 kilometers from Bandipur and is famous for its palaces, including the Mysore Palace.

Significance:

Biodiversity: Bandipur National Park is one of the most diverse wildlife sanctuaries in India, part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve. It is home to various endangered and threatened species, including tigers, elephants, leopards, dholes (wild dogs), gaurs (Indian bison), and numerous bird species.

Conservation Efforts: Bandipur has been instrumental in the conservation of the tiger population in India and actively participates in the Tiger Conservation Program.

Ecological Significance: Situated in the Western Ghats, Bandipur contributes to maintaining ecological balance and features diverse ecosystems, including moist deciduous forests, dry deciduous forests, and grassy landscapes.
