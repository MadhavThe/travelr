History and Significance:

The Nehru Zoological Park was inaugurated on October 6, 1963, by the then Prime Minister of India, Jawaharlal Nehru, after whom the park is named. It was established with the aim of conserving and protecting wildlife, promoting education and research, and providing recreation to the public.

The zoo is managed by the Telangana Forest Department and covers an extensive area of about 380 acres.

Specialties:

  • Animal Collection: The zoo is home to a diverse collection of animal species, including mammals, birds, reptiles, and amphibians. It houses over 1,500 animals representing around 150 different species.
  • White Tigers: Hyderabad Zoo is known for its successful breeding program for white tigers. Visitors have the opportunity to see these magnificent creatures up close.
  • Nocturnal House: The zoo features a Nocturnal House where visitors can observe nocturnal animals like owls, bats, and nightjars in their natural, low-light habitats.
  • Safari Park: The Nehru Zoological Park has a safari park that offers a unique experience for visitors to observe animals in a more open and naturalistic environment.

Conservation and Education:

The zoo actively participates in various conservation programs and breeding initiatives for endangered and vulnerable species. It also conducts research and educational programs to promote wildlife conservation.

The Nehru Zoological Park plays a crucial role in creating awareness about the importance of wildlife conservation and biodiversity through its educational and awareness initiatives.

Visitor Experience:

The zoo provides an engaging and educational experience for visitors of all ages. It offers guided tours, animal encounters, and interactive exhibits.

There are designated picnic areas within the zoo where families and groups can enjoy a day out in a natural setting.

The zoo has a souvenir shop and refreshment stalls for visitors.

Nearby Places:

  • Charminar: The iconic Charminar, a historic monument, is one of the most famous landmarks in Hyderabad and is located relatively close to the zoo.
  • Golconda Fort: Golconda Fort, known for its impressive architecture and historical significance, is another major attraction in Hyderabad.
  • Salar Jung Museum: The Salar Jung Museum houses a vast collection of art and historical artifacts and is a short drive from the zoo.

Location:

The Nehru Zoological Park in Hyderabad offers an opportunity for both recreation and learning. It serves as an important center for wildlife conservation and education while providing an enjoyable experience for visitors who wish to explore the diverse world of animals in a naturalistic setting.
