About Ranthambhore:

Ranthambhore National Park, located in Rajasthan, India, is a renowned wildlife reserve known for its Bengal tiger population and rich biodiversity. It offers a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and the iconic Ranthambhore Fort:

Coordinates:

Latitude: 26.0118° N

Longitude: 76.5097° E

History:

Ranthambhore Fort: The park's history is intertwined with the history of the Ranthambhore Fort, serving as a strategic stronghold for various rulers, including the Chauhans, Mughals, and Rajputs.

Wildlife Sanctuary: The park was declared a wildlife sanctuary in 1957 and later became a national park in 1980 to conserve the Bengal tiger population.

Project Tiger: In 1973, Project Tiger was launched, and Ranthambhore was included as one of the initial nine reserves under the project, emphasizing its role in tiger conservation.

Significance:

Bengal Tiger Habitat: Ranthambhore is renowned for its Bengal tiger population, making it one of the best places in India to spot these magnificent big cats in their natural habitat.

Biodiversity: The park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, making it a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers.

Historical Significance: Located around the historic Ranthambhore Fort, the park adds a unique blend of history and natural beauty to the area, dating back to the 10th century.

Nearby Places to Visit:

Ranthambhore Fort: Explore the UNESCO World Heritage Site, offering stunning views of the landscape and rich history.

Padam Talab: Visit one of the park's largest lakes for wildlife viewing.

Malik Talab: Enjoy birdwatching and wildlife observation at another picturesque lake.

Surwal Lake: Explore a serene place for birdwatching just outside the park.

Ranthambhore School of Art: Learn about traditional local art.

Kachida Valley: Spot sloth bears and leopards in this rugged terrain.

Village Tours: Experience local culture and rural life in nearby villages.
